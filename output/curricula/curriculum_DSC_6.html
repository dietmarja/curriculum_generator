Program Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Analyze complex sustainability challenges in digital environments using appropriate frameworks and methodologies
  2. Design and develop digital solutions that incorporate sustainability principles and minimize environmental impact
  3. Evaluate the sustainability performance of digital systems using appropriate metrics and assessment tools
  4. Apply ethical reasoning to address sustainability dilemmas in digital transformation contexts
  5. Collaborate effectively with multidisciplinary teams to implement sustainable digital initiatives
  6. Provide expert guidance on sustainable digital transformation aligned with organizational objectives

Work-based Learning Components

The curriculum includes the following work-based learning components:

  • Sustainable Software Engineering (5 ECTS): Implement sustainable programming practices and optimize code efficiency
  • Work-based Sustainability Project (10 ECTS): Apply sustainability knowledge in real-world workplace settings
  • Sector-Specific Digital Sustainability Applications (5 ECTS): Applying digital sustainability principles to specific industry domains
  • IoT for Environmental Monitoring (5 ECTS): Using Internet of Things technologies for sustainability monitoring and management
  • Edge Computing for Sustainability (5 ECTS): Leveraging edge computing to create efficient, low-energy sustainable systems

Curriculum Progression

This curriculum follows a progressive structure with increasing complexity:

  • Foundation Stage: Core concepts and fundamentals (early semesters)
  • Development Stage: Advanced concepts and specialized knowledge (middle semesters)
  • Application Stage: Integration, synthesis, and practical application (later semesters)

Each semester builds on the knowledge and skills from previous semesters, with prerequisites ensuring proper progression.
