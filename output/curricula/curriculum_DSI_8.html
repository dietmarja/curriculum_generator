Program Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Create new knowledge that advances the field of digital sustainability through rigorous research
  2. Develop theoretical frameworks that integrate sustainability principles with digital transformation paradigms
  3. Design and validate innovative methodologies for measuring and enhancing sustainability in digital contexts
  4. Lead innovative research addressing complex sustainability challenges at the intersection of digital technology and environmental systems
  5. Contribute to policy development and standards for sustainable digital transformation at national and international levels
  6. Advance pedagogical approaches for digital sustainability education and professional development

Work-based Learning Components

The curriculum includes the following work-based learning components:

  • Strategic Digital Sustainability Leadership (10 ECTS): Advanced work-based module on leading sustainability transitions using digital tools
  • Digital Sustainability Research Project (15 ECTS): Research-based exploration of digital sustainability challenges
  • Digital Sustainability Research Project in Practice (15 ECTS): Implementation aspects of research-based exploration of digital sustainability challenges
  • Digital Sustainability Research Project Leadership (15 ECTS): Leadership dimensions of research-based exploration of digital sustainability challenges
  • Digital Sustainability Research Project Analytics (15 ECTS): Analytical approaches to research-based exploration of digital sustainability challenges

Curriculum Progression

This curriculum follows a progressive structure with increasing complexity:

  • Foundation Stage: Core concepts and fundamentals (early semesters)
  • Development Stage: Advanced concepts and specialized knowledge (middle semesters)
  • Application Stage: Integration, synthesis, and practical application (later semesters)

Each semester builds on the knowledge and skills from previous semesters, with prerequisites ensuring proper progression.
